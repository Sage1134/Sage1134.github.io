Forging An Opportunity: What Sets Me Apart

I am excited about the opportunity to serve as next year's Mentor president. What sets me apart from the other candidates is my ability to assess a situation, adapt to it and plan accordingly.

Throughout high school, I have always wanted to pursue computer science as my post-secondary. I was told by my family “Get work experience! Get a Computer Sience Internship! That will give you a head start and set you apart from the rest”. So I tried, I tried to find an opportunity; disappointingly, I found that it is incredibly rare for a computer science position to be open to a high school student. My friends encouraged me, “Build connections! Get a Computer Science Internship!” they said. So I tried, I tried to build connections; Unfortunately strong connections were hard to come by. Then one day, I was struck with an idea: Adapt to the circumstances and create an opportunity myself. With determination, I founded a summer code camp, ByteQuest Academy. Despite the limited opportunities provided to high school students in the computer science industry, I managed to adapt and create an opportunity, an opportunity that allows me to build up aspiring computer scientists in the world of computer science.

My initiative, ByteQuest Academy, also exemplifies the other characteristics of a good mentor president: leadership, communication, organization, empathy, commitment, vision and effective role modeling. Organizing this initiative requires significant leadership abilities, as I manage the overall operations of the camp. Communication is crucial for the camp, as I work with my volunteers and participants to ensure they understand the material and feel supported. Additionally, organization is a major aspect of the initiative, as I create lesson plans and schedules, and manage camp logistics. Empathy plays a vital role in my ability to understand the diverse backgrounds and learning paces of participants, allowing me to fine-tune the lessons, to meet everyone's needs. My commitment to the camp is demonstrated through the hours spent working towards my vision for the camp: to provide free coding lessons to aspiring computer scientists. Throughout the entire initiative, I take on the position of a role model for both my volunteers and camp participants.

My experience with ByteQuest Academy is one of my many experiences that exemplifies the qualities that make me an ideal mentor president. I plan to bring the same dedication and commitment I have from ByteQuest Academy to Mentors.
